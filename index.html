  • iKON

  • What is a one off order?

    Suppose you see an item that you want, but there's no group order manager. Or there is a manager, but you don't know/trust/like them for whatever reason. Or there's a Korean site you want to order an item from, but they don't accept foreign cards. Or there is a low amount of people interested in a fansite item, but you still want to purchase it. That's where a One Off order comes in.

  • How are they different from GOs?

    The only real difference is that you pay for the shipping to the Korean address by yourself.

  • What is the process?

    1. You fill out the One Off order form with a link to your item (and what set/style/color/etc. details, if necessary)
    2. I send you an order confirmation (or a cancellation notice if it's not possible) along with details on 1st payment.
    3. You confirm the information and I send your invoice.
    4. After you pay, I order through the fansite.
    5. The rest proceeds like a normal GO in that we have to wait for the goods to finish production and arrive to my Korean address so they can be shipped.

  • How long do they take?

    The same time as regular GOs. The goods are still being produced the same way, so it's going to take just as long as a regular GO would.

  • Are the EMS fees high?

    No. Just like with regular GOs, everything is combined at my Korean address to keep EMS low for everybody.

  • Where can I see updates on my order?

    I have a tab on my masterlist specifically for One Off orders.

  • Are there any restrictions?

    I do not currently offer One Off orders to countries outside the USA, with the exception of fanclub memberships and people who already are in a regular GO with me. There are no restrictions on groups for One Off orders.

  • TWICE

  • What is a single Gmarket order?

    If you want to buy an album that there's no group order for, a face cleanser that you can't get cheaply elsewhere, Korean clothing, etc. That's what a single Gmarket order would be.

  • How are they different from regular GOs?

    I buy a bunch of different things and ship them all together, as opposed to just one type of thing (for example: ONLY buying TWICE albums).

  • What is the process?

    1. You fill out my Single Gmarket order form with a link to the item(s) you want.
    2. I check the prices and send you a confirmation email with the 1st payment details.
    3. You confirm the information and I send your 1st payment invoice. 4. I order everything together at the end of the month. If something goes out of stock, I will search for an alternative first before cancelling and refunding that part of your order.

  • How long do they take?

    I run a Gmarket GO every month for the entire month. Everything is ordered together at the end of the month after all payments are received. I do not order items individually unless the shipping for that item is free anyway.

  • Are the EMS fees high?

    No. Just like with regular GOs, everything is combined at my Korean address to keep EMS low for everybody.

  • Where can I see updates on my order?

    I have a tab on my masterlist specifically for Single Gmarket orders.

  • Are there any restrictions?

    I cannot get any of the following items, either because international shipping is banned or because Korgou does not have proper storage:

    Power banks or anything with a lithium battery
    Perfume
    Nail polish
    Hair dye
    Perishable food or drink

  • INFINITE

  • How do I pay?

    Paypal is the only method I accept. I will send you invoices through Paypal for all payments.

  • Why do I have to use the invoice?

    This is to protect my account from being locked like other GOMs have had happen to them in the past. I also used to always have a few people in every order pay through Goods and Services WITHOUT telling me about it, so I've eliminated that problem for myself.

  • Do I have to pay right away after submitting a form?

    No. You can fill out the form on the 1st day I open an order and pay on the last day if you want. In fact, I prefer that you fill out the form as soon as possible because it is SO much harder to add someone last minute than it is to already have your information in the spreadsheet.

  • What happens if I miss a payment?

    Miss a first payment = Your order will be cancelled.

    Miss a second (Korean address shipping)/third (EMS) payment = You get a 12 hour grace period after the deadline, then your order is cancelled. You will not get a refund UNLESS you can find someone to take your spot because now I'm going to have to pay your share of the EMS fee.

    Miss a fourth (domestic) payment = Your goods will be resold. You will not get a refund UNTIL THEY SELL, and there will be a 10% service fee deducted from the amount because you wasted my time and treated my home like your personal storage unit.

  • I don't want the item anymore, can I get a refund?

    Before 1st payment deadline = Yes. It's best to do it sooner rather than later, though. If you give me less than 3 days notice, that's a strike against you. If you do this 3 times, you will be banned from ordering with me in the future. You should be committed to an order when you sign up. I understand emergencies and unexpected things can happen, but it is still your responsibility to take care of things ASAP and not wait until the last minute.

    After 1st payment deadline = ONLY if you can find someone to buy your spot.

  • Can I pay in installments?

    If you have ordered with me at least THREE times and have no strikes on your record, yes. I will set up your invoices to allow installment payments.

  • BTOB

  • How much will the EMS/DHL fee be?

    I don't know. It depends on the weight of the item, the total number of orders, what other items are at the Korean address to be shipped at the same time, etc.

  • How much is domestic (USA) shipping?

    This varies depending on the type of item and shipping method, but I send most orders in a Priority Flat Rate Padded envelope which is $7.20. Other shipping methods that I use vary in cost based on weight and/or distance from me to you.

  • How much is international shipping?

    I ship everything internationally directly from my Korean address (except slogans and other light items for Canadian orders). It varies by country, item weight/size, shipping method used, etc. so I cannot give you a cookie cutter answer for it.

  • How do you ship goods?

    Most orders are sent in a Priority Flat Rate Padded envelope.
    If your order is too large for that, I usually send in a Medium Flat Rate Box.
    For albums, I always try to send through Media Mail.
    Unfolded posters I send through First Class or Priority 2 Day, depending on the tube weight.

  • Can I combine my orders?

    If they're expected to arrive within a month of each other, yes. I don't have a ton of space to store items, so I can only keep items for this long before I need them out.

  • When will my order ship?

    I work a variable schedule, so whenever I find the time to ship, that's when it gets done. If you need something by a certain date (like for a gift), please let me know so I can prioritize your shipment! Do not abuse this system though, it will get you a strike on your record.

  • Can I change my shipping address?

    Yes. I always send address confirmations before I even send out domestic invoices, so you will have a chance to change your address if you need to.

  • Miscellaneous

  • How can I become a GOM? Do you have any tips?

    First off, I only recommend becoming a GOM for fandoms that don't have any or only have a small amount. I promise you it's just gonna stress you out if you try to jump into a big fandom like EXO or BTS where there's already a million managers and some of them are REALLY territorial about certain group members and/or fansites. Also being new and dealing with the mass amounts of buyers in those fandoms is probably not the best way to start your GOM life because of the high stress levels. But that's just my opinion! So if you really wanna do it that way, then go for it.

    Deadlines are important. The fansite will always put them in KST, so make sure you check what that is in your timezone and adjust your own deadlines accordingly. You want to leave yourself enough time to make the payments and in case any last minute issues pop up, like a dropout or a late payment etc. Sometimes the fansites will close orders earlier too, so be mindful of that.

    Also, Google spreadsheets and forms are your best friend, I promise.
